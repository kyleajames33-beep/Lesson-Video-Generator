## Lesson-by-lesson video plan

Every row maps to an existing site lesson. The "anchor / hook" column is taken from the audit doc and is non-negotiable — students see it in the written lesson, so the video must use the same one.

### IQ1 — Predicting products of chemical reactions

#### L1. Physical & Chemical Change
- **Site path:** `module3/lesson01.html`
- **Hook anchor:** "Is a new substance formed?" (the five indicators of chemical change)
- **Core idea:** Distinguish physical change (rearrangement of state) from chemical change (new substance formed)
- **Likely worked example:** Classify a list of changes — burning magnesium, dissolving salt, melting ice, rusting iron
- **Misconception target:** "Dissolving makes a new substance" → no, it's a physical change (no bonds broken)
- **Reusable visual cue:** Five indicators SVG (pull from site as design reference)
- **Decision rule:** "Ask: have new bonds formed?"

#### L2. Synthesis & Decomposition
- **Site path:** `module3/lesson02.html`
- **Hook anchor:** Beirut 2020 ammonium nitrate disaster (controlled vs explosive decomposition)
- **Core idea:** Two foundational reaction patterns: A + B → AB (synthesis), AB → A + B (decomposition)
- **Likely worked example:** Predict products of `2Na + Cl₂ → ?` and `CaCO₃ → ?` (heated)
- **Misconception target:** "Decomposition always needs heat" → no, some decompose spontaneously (e.g. H₂O₂)
- **Reusable visual cue:** Side-by-side A+B↔AB SVG comparison
- **Decision rule:** "One reactant → decomposition. Two reactants joining → synthesis."

#### L3. Precipitation & Solubility Rules
- **Site path:** `module3/lesson03.html`
- **Hook anchor:** Barium meal contrast agent (BaSO₄ insoluble — safe to swallow despite barium toxicity)
- **Core idea:** NAGSAG solubility framework + writing molecular / full ionic / net ionic equations
- **Likely worked example:** Predict precipitate of AgNO₃(aq) + NaCl(aq); write all three equation forms
- **Misconception target:** "All ionic compounds are soluble" → use NAGSAG to override intuition
- **Real-world thread:** Water treatment (Pb²⁺/Hg²⁺ removal)
- **Decision rule:** "Identify spectator ions. Drop them. What remains is the net ionic equation."

#### L4. Combustion Reactions
- **Site path:** `module3/lesson04.html`
- **Hook anchor:** Bushfire chemistry — fire front (complete) vs smouldering zone (incomplete, CO production)
- **Core idea:** Complete combustion → CO₂ + H₂O; incomplete → CO + C + H₂O. CO toxicity from 200× haemoglobin affinity
- **Likely worked example:** Balance complete combustion of butane (C₄H₁₀); compare to incomplete
- **Misconception target:** "Incomplete combustion produces dangerous fumes randomly" → no, the products are predictable from O₂ stoichiometry
- **Decision rule:** "Enough O₂ → CO₂. Limited O₂ → CO and/or C."

#### L5. Acid-Base & Acid-Carbonate Reactions
- **Site path:** `module3/lesson05.html`
- **Hook anchor:** Antacid formulations — Mg(OH)₂ (smooth) vs CaCO₃ (gas-producing burp)
- **Core idea:** Universal net ionic for strong acid+base: H⁺ + OH⁻ → H₂O. Acid+carbonate: SWC mnemonic (Salt + Water + CO₂)
- **Likely worked example:** Predict products of HCl + NaOH and HCl + Na₂CO₃; write balanced equations
- **Misconception target:** "Acid + carbonate produces water only" → no, you also get CO₂ gas (the burp)
- **Decision rule:** "Carbonate in the reactant → expect CO₂ in the product."

#### L6. Indigenous Detoxification & Balancing Equations
- **Site path:** `module3/lesson06.html`
- **Hook anchor:** Aboriginal/Torres Strait Islander cycad detoxification — leaching cycasin via running water
- **Core idea:** Concentration gradient drives extraction (physical step) + fermentation/roasting (chemical step). Tie back to balancing equations.
- **Likely worked example:** Balance a multi-step combustion or acid+carbonate equation following the leaching analogy of "checking each side"
- **Misconception target:** "Indigenous knowledge is pre-scientific" → no, the technique exhibits empirical understanding of solubility and concentration gradients
- **Decision rule:** "Balance: count atoms each side. Adjust coefficients. Re-check."
- **Note:** This lesson combines a syllabus-mandated cultural-context dot point with a balancing-equation skill — keep both threads.

### IQ2 — Predicting metal reactivity & electrochemistry

#### L7. Metal Activity Series & Reactions of Metals
- **Site path:** `module3/lesson07.html`
- **Hook anchor:** Sydney Harbour Bridge zinc-coated steel (sacrificial protection)
- **Core idea:** Build the activity series from observed reactions with water/acid/oxygen; passivation masks aluminium's true reactivity
- **Likely worked example:** Predict whether Mg(s) + Zn²⁺(aq) → reaction, vs Cu(s) + Zn²⁺(aq) → no reaction
- **Misconception target:** "Aluminium is unreactive because it doesn't fizz in acid" → it's actually reactive but passivated by Al₂O₃
- **Decision rule:** "More reactive metal displaces less reactive metal."
- **Sets up:** L8 (electron transfer formalism), L9 (reduction potentials), L10 (predicting cells)

#### L8. Redox Reactions & Oxidation States
- **Site path:** `module3/lesson08.html`
- **Hook anchor:** Household bleach (OCl⁻) as an oxidant — proves redox isn't just "oxygen"
- **Core idea:** Redox = electron transfer. OIL RIG. Six oxidation-number rules. Half-equation balancing in acidic solution (H₂O, H⁺).
- **Likely worked example:** Determine oxidation states in MnO₄⁻; balance half-equation MnO₄⁻ → Mn²⁺
- **Misconception target:** "Redox always involves oxygen" → counter with bleach example (OCl⁻ oxidises without donating O)
- **Decision rule:** "Oxidation = lose electrons (LEO) / oxidation number rises. Reduction = gain (GER) / oxidation number falls."

#### L9. Galvanic Cells & Standard Reduction Potentials
- **Site path:** `module3/lesson09.html`
- **Hook anchor:** "Why does a battery die?" — four-component model (anode, cathode, salt bridge, external circuit)
- **Core idea:** Spontaneous redox harnessed for electrical work. E°cell = E°cathode − E°anode. Salt bridge maintains charge neutrality.
- **Likely worked example:** Calculate E°cell for Zn|Zn²⁺ || Cu²⁺|Cu using standard reduction potentials
- **Misconception target:** "The salt bridge carries electrons" → no, it carries ions to maintain neutrality; electrons go through the wire
- **Decision rule:** "E°cell > 0 → spontaneous. E°cell < 0 → not spontaneous (needs energy in)."

#### L10. Galvanic Cells — Inert Electrodes & Predicting Reactions
- **Site path:** `module3/lesson10.html`
- **Hook anchor:** Cathodic protection — sacrificial anodes on ship hulls / pipelines
- **Core idea:** Inert electrodes (Pt, graphite) when both species are aqueous/gaseous. Use E° vs 0.00 V (H⁺/H₂) to predict acid solubility of metals.
- **Likely worked example:** Predict whether Cu(s) reacts with dilute HCl; compare to Zn(s) with dilute HCl
- **Misconception target:** "Every metal reacts with acid" → only metals with E° < 0 V (above hydrogen in reduction-potential terms)
- **Decision rule:** "If a metal's E° is more negative than H⁺/H₂ (0.00 V), it dissolves in dilute acid."

### IQ3 — Rates of reaction

#### L11. Collision Theory & Reaction Rate
- **Site path:** `module3/lesson11.html`
- **Hook anchor:** Glow stick — temperature changes rate (brightness) but not total light produced
- **Core idea:** Effective collision = sufficient energy (≥Eₐ) AND correct orientation. Activation energy vs ΔH on energy diagrams.
- **Likely worked example:** Read an energy diagram — identify Eₐ, ΔH, transition state; predict whether reaction is exo- or endothermic
- **Misconception target:** "Higher temperature means more product" → no, higher T speeds rate but doesn't change yield (at constant equilibrium)
- **Decision rule:** "Effective collision needs both: enough energy and right orientation."

#### L12. Factors Affecting Reaction Rate
- **Site path:** `module3/lesson12.html`
- **Hook anchor:** Catalytic converter cold-start problem — why your car pollutes most in the first minute
- **Core idea:** Maxwell-Boltzmann shifts with T (temperature effect); catalysts lower Eₐ threshold; surface area / concentration affect collision frequency
- **Likely worked example:** Compare two trials (varied T, varied catalyst presence) on a rate-vs-time graph; explain shape difference using collision theory
- **Misconception target:** "Catalysts speed up by giving energy" → no, they provide an alternative pathway with lower Eₐ
- **Decision rule:** "Each rate factor changes either collision frequency or collision effectiveness — name which."

## Resolved decisions

1. **Lesson length target** — 5–10 min per lesson. Denser electrochemistry lessons (L8–L10) and multi-step worked-example lessons can run up to 10 min; conceptual lessons stay around 6. Never compress below 5 — that's a sign content is too thin and should merge with another lesson.

2. **Checkpoint videos** — Yes, build them, but **not as quiz reviews** (the site already has interactive quizzes; video review can't beat 10 quiz reps for retention). Build them as **synthesis "boss fight" videos** instead: one per IQ cluster (3 total for M3), 8–10 min each, showing the *decision tree* for picking the right method across the cluster's lessons. Format:
   - **Cold open:** 4 mixed scenarios shown back-to-back, no answers
   - **Decision tree:** map each scenario type to the method (which lesson taught it)
   - **3 worked walkthroughs:** each scenario solved with explicit method choice + the discriminator question
   - **Exam pitfall montage:** the 3 misconceptions most likely to show up as distractors across this IQ
   - **Closing decision rule:** one sentence the student carries into the exam
   These are the "transfer + integration" videos the quiz can't be. Add them to the production order as **Phase 6** (after all 12 lessons ship): Checkpoint-1 (covers L1–L6), Checkpoint-2 (L7–L10), Checkpoint-3 (L11–L12 + cross-IQ ties).

3. **L6 cultural context (cycad detoxification)** — Pull voiceover wording **verbatim from the site lesson** for any sentence that touches on Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander practices, naming, or cultural framing. The site copy was presumably reviewed; redrafting risks getting nuance wrong even with good intent. Use fresh phrasing only for the balancing-equations skill thread that wraps the lesson. Mark verbatim sections in the JSON with a comment like `// VERBATIM from site lesson — do not paraphrase`.

4. **Asset directory convention** — `public/assets/hscscience/chem-y11-m3/<lessonId>/<asset>.png`. Mirroring the site lesson path makes future site→video asset sync trivial. Register each asset in `src/assets/index.ts` as before. The existing `public/assets/hscscience/` folder (already untracked in git status) is the right root.
