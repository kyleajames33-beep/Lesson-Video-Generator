# Gold Standard Video Standard

This is the target for HSCScience lesson videos. The goal is not to copy Atomi, Cognito, or YouTube creators. The goal is to beat them on HSC specificity, worked-example clarity, mistake prevention, and integration with revision.

## Competitor Benchmark

Public competitor signals:

- Atomi positions itself around curriculum-aligned videos, quizzes, feedback, revision recommendations, and teacher tools.
- Atomi's Stage 6 Chemistry guidance emphasises HSC-specific lessons, challenge questions, quizzes, text lessons, exam application videos, and practical use in flipped learning.
- Cognito positions itself around short videos, notes, quizzes, flashcards, exam-style questions, AI feedback, progress tracking, and high-volume student use.
- YouTube competitors win on personality, speed, thumbnails, and free discovery, but they usually lose on structured revision, syllabus tracking, and platform memory.

## What We Need To Beat

Atomi does well:

- Short, direct videos.
- Curriculum alignment.
- Quizzes and feedback around the video.
- A complete school-facing platform.
- Clear positioning for teachers and students.

Cognito does well:

- Simple explanations.
- Short lesson flow.
- Notes plus videos plus questions.
- Strong student habit loop.
- Progress and revision tools around the content.

YouTube does well:

- Strong hooks.
- Human energy.
- Fast visual payoff.
- Searchable topic coverage.
- Clear thumbnails and titles.

HSCScience should beat them by combining:

- NESA-specific lesson mapping.
- HSC-style worked examples.
- Common mistake prevention.
- Quick checks directly after explanations.
- Transcript, quiz, notes, and revision links on the same lesson page.
- A production memory that improves each new video.

## Score Targets

Every reference lesson should target 10/10 in these categories:

| Category | 10/10 means |
| --- | --- |
| Video presentation | Polished visual hierarchy, readable chemistry notation, purposeful animation, no clutter, no dead screen time. |
| Repeatability | A new lesson can be generated from the standard with consistent structure, required fields, validation, transcripts, and manifests. |
| Production efficiency | The pipeline catches problems before rendering or voiceover, and common exports are one command. |
| Engagement | Hook, visual build, active recall, worked example, misconception, and transfer prompt all appear naturally. |
| Student likeability | A Year 11 student feels the video is clear, useful, not childish, and directly helpful for marks. |

## Non-Negotiables

- Start with a student problem, not a syllabus heading.
- Every scene must teach one idea.
- Every worked example must show `Known`, `Find`, `Formula`, `Substitute`, and `Answer`.
- Every calculation-heavy lesson must include unit logic.
- Every lesson must include at least one quick check.
- Every lesson must include at least one common mistake or misconception.
- Summary must finish with a decision rule, not a vague recap.
- Voiceover must fit scene timing before audio is generated.
- Site delivery must never preload videos on index pages.

## How To Beat YouTube

Do not compete with YouTube only as a video. Compete as a learning loop:

1. Watch the short explanation.
2. See the exact worked method.
3. Answer a quick check.
4. Review the common mistake.
5. Open the transcript or notes.
6. Send the weak point into revision.

The paid value is not only the MP4. The paid value is that HSCScience knows what the student has watched, what they got wrong, and what they should revise next.

## Characters And Storyline

Do not add full cartoon characters by default. They add production cost and can make Year 11 content feel younger than it should.

Use lightweight recurring roles instead:

- `Student problem`: the opening confusion.
- `Teacher move`: the explanation or method choice.
- `Exam trap`: the misconception.
- `Check yourself`: the active recall moment.

This gives story structure without needing animated characters.
