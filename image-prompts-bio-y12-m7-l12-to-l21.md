# Image generation prompts — Biology Year 12 Module 7 (Infectious Disease), Lessons 12–21

One hero image per lesson, used on the hook scene. Every image MUST be a **transparent PNG with a real alpha channel** (no white, off-white, or coloured background fill). **No baked-in text, labels, numbers, captions, arrows-with-words, or letterforms of any kind** — all on-screen wording is rendered by the Remotion layer. Vector / flat-illustration style, clean editorial science aesthetic, generous negative space so the subject reads at small size. Centred subject, no hard cropping at edges.

House style (apply to all): modern flat vector illustration with soft cell-shading, a restrained palette built around deep teal/emerald greens and indigo with a single warm amber accent, subtle drop shadow, no gradients-as-background, no photoreal skin/gore, classroom-appropriate. Aspect roughly 4:3, subject occupying the central ~70%.

## L12 — `bioM7L12HivTHelper`
**Concept:** HIV destroying the CD4+ helper T cell, the coordinator of adaptive immunity.

**Prompt:**
Flat vector science illustration of a single large immune cell (a helper T cell) shown as a rounded teal cell with a few short surface receptor stalks, being approached and attacked by several small spiky indigo virus particles (HIV virions) with knob-tipped surface spikes. The cell looks compromised, with one virion fusing to its surface. Around the helper cell, three faint, greyed-out smaller cells drift away losing their glow, suggesting other immune cells going quiet without their coordinator. Clean, didactic, soft cell-shaded style, deep teal and indigo with a single amber highlight on the point of viral contact. Transparent background with alpha channel. No text, no letters, no numbers, no labels, no arrows.
